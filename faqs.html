Fabric Care

We recommend hand washing lawn suits in cold water with a mild detergent. If machine washing, use the delicate cycle at 30°C or below. Avoid wringing — gently squeeze out excess water and lay flat to dry in the shade. Direct sunlight can cause colours to fade prematurely.

Embroidered and heavily embellished formal suits should be dry cleaned only to preserve the thread work and zari details. If spot cleaning at home, use a damp cloth with gentle, circular motions. Never soak embroidered fabric as this can cause threads to loosen and colours to bleed.

Store your suits in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Use breathable fabric garment bags rather than plastic covers to prevent moisture build-up. For heavier bridal and formal pieces, fold them carefully with acid-free tissue paper to maintain the shape of embroidery and embellishments.

Yes, but with care. Always iron chiffon dupattas inside-out on the lowest heat setting. Use a pressing cloth between the iron and the fabric to avoid direct contact. Steam ironing is preferable and most effective for removing creases. Never iron embroidered sections — simply hang them in a steamy bathroom to let wrinkles fall out naturally.
